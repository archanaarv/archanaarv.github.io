About Me

About Me

I am a third-year Ph.D. candidate at the University of California, Riverside, where I work with Prof. Gabriela Canalizo to study black hole activities in dwarf galaxies. I am interested in understanding the nature of low-mass black holes and their effect on host dwarf galaxies using observational techniques in the visible and near-infrared regions of the electromagnetic spectrum.

Before my doctoral research, I did a five-year Integrated Bachelor+Master of Science in Physics from Pondicherry University in India. For my undergraduate and master's research, I dabbled in Radio Astronomy, Astronomical Software, Solar Physics, and Active Galactic Nuclei (AGN).

Apart from my research, I enjoy teaching, engaging in science communication. and doing astronomy outreach. I am also passionate about science policy, and I hope to get more scientists involved in the government to make informed decisions on the policies that affect all of us.

Research

Research

Outflows in Dwarf galaxies

Gas outflows in dwarf galaxies play an essential role in regulating feedback in dwarf galaxies and helping us to understand star formation in them. We studied ionized gas outflows in dwarf galaxies to determine if there are any significant differences between the energetics of stellar-powered and AGN-powered outflows. We find that AGN-driven outflows are more energetic than stellar-powered outflows. Through this work, we hope to provide the motivation to include more models of AGN feedback in dwarf galaxy simulations.

Detecting Intermediate-Mass Black Holes in Dwarf Galaxies using Variability

Detecting low-mass black holes in dwarf galaxies has always been challenging due to their weak signatures that often get mixed with stars. We explore whether mid-infrared variability is a better indicator of black holes, particularly in the lower-mass range.

Searching for signatures of strong nuclear activity using coronal lines

Coronal lines are forbidden lines caused by highly energetic processes. They are commonly believed to be an indicative signature of black holes. I use high-resolution single-slit spectroscopy and Integral Field Spectroscopy to search for such lines and identify potential signs of black holes in a low-metallicity galaxy J0944, commonly called SB2.

Dual Active Galactic Nuclei

Observing black holes merge has been a challenge, as the galaxy at that stage will be enshrouded in dust and gas. We use a novel technique incorporating galaxies' variability across different observational bands to detect dual merging AGNs using Integral field spectroscopy in the infrared.

Advocacy

Advocacy

Science always inspires me and fills me with wonder. I try to advocate for better practices and culture in science to make it more accessible and easier for people to pursue a career in fundamental science research. Here are some of the ways I try to achieve this:

Science Outreach

I regularly help design and demonstrate affordable and easy-to-replicate astronomy experiments for K-12 children as a part of Camp Highlander at UCR. I also help with telescope viewings for middle school students in Riverside County.

Science Communication

I actively try to communicate science to a general audience to foster interest and make it more accessible. Check out my work for Astrobites, a collaboration of astronomy graduate students who take scientific papers and condense them down into bite-sized pieces.


Listen to me talking about my research on black holes and why we need to have faith in astrophysics as a guest on Cosmic Voice, a podcast by Spaceonova.

Science Policy

I am passionate about communicating science to policymakers and advocating for data-driven, evidence-based policy decisions through my involvement with the UCR Science to Policy group.


Check out my policy brief on Anticipatory Diplomacy in Outer Space, published by the Federation of American Scientists.

Science Equity

I am the current Vice-President of POWUR at UCR. Through this group, we hope to create a safe and inclusive space for womxn and minorities in the Physics and Astronomy department at UCR. I host monthly Womxn in Physics coffee socials and have run Book Clubs with high school Physics teachers to explore and discuss the relevance of STEM education to women.
