About Me

About Me

I am a third-year Ph.D. candidate at the University of California, Riverside. I am an observational astronomer and I study black hole activities in small galaxies using Keck telescope. I am broadly interested in understanding the nature of low-mass black holes and the effect they have on their host dwarf galaxies. I work primarily with data in the visible and the near-infrared region of the electromagnetic spectrum. I am also interested in observational techniques such as Integral Field Spectroscopy and how to better use them to capture black hole activities in small galaxies.

Prior to my doctoral research, I did a five year Integrated Master of Science in Physics from Pondicherry University in India. For my undergraduate and masters research, I dabbled in Radio Astronomy, Astronomical Software, Solar Physics and Active Galactic Nuclei. I enjoyed working with AGNs for my Masters Thesis and continued to work on it for my Ph.D.

Apart from my research, I enjoy teaching, engaging in science communication. and doing astronomy outreach. I am also passionate about science policy and I hope to get more scientists involved in the government to make informed decisions on the policies that affect all of us.

Research

Research

Outflows in Dwarf galaxies

Gas outflows in dwarf galaxies play an important role in regulating feedback in dwarf galaxies and helping us to understand star formation in them. Stellar feedback has long believed to be sufficient, but the recent increase in the discovery of AGNs in dwarf galaxies lead us to reconsider if AGN feedback could be more relevant. We study ionized gas outflows in dwarf galaxies to determine if there are any significant differences between the energetics of stellar-powered and AGN-powered outflows. We find that AGN-driven outflows are more energetic than stellar-powered outflows.

Detecting Intermediate-Mass Black Holes in Dwarf Galaxies using Variability

Detecting lower mass black holes in dwarf galaxies has always been a challenge, due to their weak signatures that often gets mixed with stars. We try to explore if mid-infrared variability is a better indicator of black holes, particularly in the lower-mass range.

    A Comparison of Outflow Properties in AGN Dwarfs vs. Star Forming Dwarfs (Archana Aravindan et al 2023 ApJ 950 33)
